Kactus Food Delivery

Project Overview

Brief Introduction

KACTUS is an online food ordering platform aimed at assisting busy individuals who are unable to prepare meals for themselves but desire quality food with prompt delivery. The application was crafted with users' requirements in mind, and its user-friendly features have been beneficial to many in managing their daily meals.

Duration

8 Weeks

Role

UI/UX Designer

Platform

Mobile

Tools

Figma Adobe XD Adobe Photoshop

Goals

The purpose of the KACTUS Delivery App is to offer customers a hassle-free and effective means of ordering food from nearby eateries and receiving doorstep delivery. By providing a user-friendly interface, the app enables users to browse through restaurant menus, personalize their orders and make payments with ease. With a focus on prompt and dependable food delivery, the app also offers real-time order status updates to enhance the customer experience.

Objectives

  1. To provide customers with a convenient and efficient way to order food from local restaurants and have it delivered to their doorstep.
  2. To offer a user-friendly interface that allows customers to browse menus, customize orders, and pay for their food with ease.
  3. To ensure timely and reliable food delivery by partnering with trusted restaurants and delivery drivers.
  4. To provide real-time order tracking and updates, so customers can stay informed about the status of their food delivery.
  5. To offer a wide selection of restaurants and menu items to cater to a variety of customer preferences.
  6. To provide personalized recommendations to customers based on their past orders and preferences.

Audiences

  • Busy professionals who may not have the time to cook or visit a restaurant during the day.
  • College students who may prefer to order food to their dorm or apartment instead of cooking or eating at the dining hall.
  • Families with young children who may prefer the convenience of ordering food instead of going out to eat with their kids.
  • Seniors or individuals with disabilities who may have difficulty traveling to restaurants or cooking for themselves.
  • Food enthusiasts who enjoy exploring different cuisines and trying out new restaurants.
  • Tourists who may not be familiar with the local dining options and prefer the convenience of ordering food through an app.

Design Process

User Research

Qualitative Research

To gain further insights into food delivery, I conducted 6 interviews for each category (students, employees, and others) in qualitative research. During these interviews, we delved into their positive and negative experiences to gain a deeper understanding of their perspectives.

Questions:

  • Can you walk me through the process of ordering food through the app?
  • What do you like and dislike about the food delivery app?
  • What are some of the challenges you face when using the app to order food?
  • How important is the user interface and design of the app to you?
  • Can you describe a recent experience you had using the app to order food?

Quantitative Research

I utilized Google Forms to generate online survey questions aimed at users. The survey garnered roughly 80 responses, enabling us to accurately define the problem at hand.

Online Survey

Competitive Analysis

Upon completing the survey, I generated certain ideas for my application. However, to gain a better understanding of the market dynamics, I deemed it necessary to conduct a thorough analysis of potential competitors, their respective strengths, and weaknesses. As a result of my research, I have determined that both direct and indirect applications possess the potential to be strong competitors in the current market.

Pros

  • Large selection, clients have the opportunities to order different type of food.
  • Real-time tracking, ability to track the delivery person.

Cons

  • Lack of different type of payment, clients want a feature to choose their payment method.
  • Poor User Interface.

User Persona

Utilizing the feedback obtained from the user interview and survey, I developed a user persona for my application. This persona provides me with a comprehensive understanding of the user's goals and challenges, enabling me to tailor the application to better serve their needs.

Busy Professional

Name:

Sarah

Age:

35

Occupation:

Marketing Manager

Daily issue:

Sarah has a busy schedule and often works long hours. She finds it difficult to make time to prepare meals at home, and frequently ends up skipping meals or eating unhealthy fast food.

Goals and motivations:

Sarah wants to maintain a healthy lifestyle and eat nutritious meals, but struggles to find the time to do so.

Pain points:

Limited time for meal preparation, difficulty finding healthy food options, frequent reliance on fast food or takeout, feeling guilty for not making healthier choices.

College Student

Name:

Alex

Age:

21

Occupation:

College Student

Daily issue:

Alex has a tight budget and limited time between classes, studying, and part-time work. He often skips meals or eats cheap, unhealthy food due to time and financial constraints.

Goals and motivations:

Alex wants to maintain a healthy diet and have enough energy to get through his busy schedule, but struggles to find affordable, healthy food options.

Pain points:

Limited budget for food, limited time for meal preparation, difficulty finding healthy food options on campus or nearby, feeling tired and sluggish due to poor diet.

Empathy Map

By conversing with the user, I gained insight into their thought process and was able to empathize with them. Using this understanding, I developed an empathy map to articulate their perspective.

Says

  • I'm so hungry, I need food now.
  • I don't want to spend too much time looking for a place to eat.
  • I want something healthy and nutritious to eat.
  • I wish there were more options for food delivery in my area.
  • I want to know exactly when my food will arrive.

Thinks

  • I hope the food delivery app is easy to use.
  • I wonder if I can find a good deal on my meal.
  • I hope the food will be fresh and tasty.
  • I hope the delivery person will be friendly and professional.
  • I hope the app has good customer support in case something goes wrong.

does

  • Searches for food delivery options online.
  • Scans menus and restaurant reviews on the app.
  • Places an order for food delivery.
  • Tracks the delivery person's progress on the app.
  • Leaves a review of the food and delivery experience.

Feels

  • Hungry and impatient.
  • Excited to try new food options.
  • Anxious about the quality of the food and delivery experience.
  • Satisfied and relieved when the food arrives.
  • Disappointed or frustrated if something goes wrong with the delivery or food quality.
